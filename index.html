What Noisebridge is

Noisebridge is a volunteer-run hackerspace and a 501(c)(3) nonprofit in San Francisco. It's a shared, member-supported space where people make things — electronics, software, textiles, art, food, music — teach each other, and keep the doors open for the next person who's curious.

There is no boss. Noisebridge runs as a do-ocracy: the people who do the work make the decisions, and consensus is how we settle the rest. Our one rule is the whole social contract: be excellent to each other.

Support Noisebridge

Donations keep the space alive — rent, tools, utilities, and an open door that asks nothing of the people who walk through it. As a 501(c)(3), gifts to Noisebridge are tax-deductible in the US.

History

Noisebridge opened in 2008 and has been a fixture of San Francisco's maker and free-culture communities ever since — thousands of classes, projects, and people through the door.
