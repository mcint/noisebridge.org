# Contributing to noisebridge.org

This site belongs to Noisebridge and the people who show up — **not to any one
person**. It runs like the space does: a *do-ocracy*. If you see something thin,
wrong, or missing, that's an opening. Fix it.

## Two easy ways in

1. **Open an issue** — suggest content, point at a hole, or report something broken.
   No code needed. Templates will walk you through it.
2. **Open a pull request** — edit the site directly. It's plain HTML + CSS, no build
   step, so the barrier is low:
   ```sh
   # see your change instantly
   open index.html        # or: python3 -m http.server
   ```

## Good first contributions — pick a hole

The site ships with **marked holes** (the dashed, highlighted blocks). Each is an
invitation:

- Photos of the space and its projects
- How to visit / hours / getting in
- The guilds (sewing, laser, electronics, …) and what they do
- A real history timeline (pull from the wiki and archives)
- The donate flow, recurring option, and a "where your money goes" breakdown
- Grants & larger funding — what we'd build with it

## Keep it simple

- Plain, semantic HTML and minimal CSS. No frameworks, no build step (for now).
- Readable and accessible: real headings, alt text on images, good contrast.
- Match the existing voice — warm, plain, a little irreverent.

## Bigger proposals

Changing *what the site says or does* (positioning, structure, what belongs on `.org`
vs the community `.net` or tech `.io`) is welcome too — bring it to the Noisebridge
[wiki](https://www.noisebridge.net/) and the open proposal process so it's decided in
the open. A concern that isn't adopted here may find a better home on `.net` or `.io`.

## The one rule

**Be excellent to each other.** See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## License

Contribution terms / site license: **to be decided** (a hole of its own). By opening a
PR you're offering your contribution to Noisebridge under whatever open license the
project settles on.
