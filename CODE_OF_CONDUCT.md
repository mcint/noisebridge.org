# Be excellent to each other

That's the whole rule, and it has carried Noisebridge for a long time. It asks you to
assume good faith, share generously, clean up after yourself, and treat the people
around you — and the next person through the door — with respect.

In practice, here, that means:

- **Welcome newcomers.** Everyone was new once. Answer the "dumb" question kindly.
- **Credit the work, not the ego.** This site belongs to the community. Don't claim
  collective work as your own; do lift up what others have done.
- **Disagree in the open, generously.** Bring real disagreements to the
  [wiki](https://www.noisebridge.net/) and the open proposal process. Argue the idea,
  not the person.
- **Harassment, discrimination, and intimidation are not excellent** and are not
  welcome — in issues, pull requests, or anywhere else.

## If something isn't excellent

Raise it. For conduct concerns, the Noisebridge community process (wiki, meetings, and
the people who run them) is the place to bring it. Don't suffer in silence, and don't
let a bad moment go unaddressed.
